## What happened (root cause)

Two failure modes compounded:

1. **Stale orphan swift processes from prior fires.** On entry to Step 2 P1 (drain wip queue), three swift-test/xctest PIDs were already alive from earlier today: `swift-test 84672` (4:10PM), `swift-test 90907 --filter MainAppEmptyTests` (4:18PM), and `xctest 85518` (4:11PM). The 4:18 process had been wedged for 1h32m. SwiftPM serializes — running `swift test` while another instance holds `.build` locks blocks indefinitely.

2. **Buffered tail pipeline.** First test invocation was `swift test 2>&1 | tail -25` inside a backgrounded Bash. `tail -25` only emits its window once stdin closes (i.e., when swift-test exits). Combined with (1), nothing was being written to the output file, and I burned ~10 × 10-min `TaskOutput` waits trying to confirm progress that wasn't happening.

By the time I diagnosed and killed the orphans, the elapsed clock was already ~100 min. Next fire was inside 15 min, and the rules forbid pushing to main without a green test run. Ship-nothing was the correct call.

## Lessons for the next fire (and for AGENTS.md)

1. **Step 1.5 — always sweep stale swift processes before doing anything else.**
   ```bash
   pkill -9 -f "swift-test|xctest" 2>/dev/null
   sleep 2
   ps aux | grep -E "swift-test|xctest" | grep -v grep && echo "STILL ALIVE — investigate" || echo "clean"
   ```
   This must be the very first thing after Step 1 sync. A wedged xctest from a prior fire silently kills the entire fire because SwiftPM serializes on `.build`.

2. **Never run `swift test 2>&1 | tail -N` in background.** `tail` buffers until EOF. Use `swift test 2>&1 | tee /tmp/swift_test.log` or stream raw — the test runner already prints incrementally.

3. **TaskOutput timeouts are budget.** A single 10-min wait that finds nothing useful is 10 min you can't get back. After ~2 cycles of "still no output", switch to inspecting the process tree directly (`ps aux | grep swift`) — that's near-instant.

4. **GlobalHotkeyContractTests may be flaky under headless xctest.** Two of its cases started but never reported pass/fail before the pipeline was aborted. Could be a real deadlock on NSApp/NSWindow without a foreground GUI session, or could be misattribution because of a different upstream wedge. The next fire that gets a clean test run should watch this suite specifically; if it reproduces, file as REP-NEW alongside the ContactsResolver carve-out.
