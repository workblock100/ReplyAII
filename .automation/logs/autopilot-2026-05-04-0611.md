## Strategy this fire — why so much errorDescription pinning?

Six error enums in the codebase surface their `errorDescription` strings
verbatim into user-facing UI (inbox banner, Settings → Channels). Only two
had direct tests of the `errorDescription` per-case copy. A careless edit
to the enum could silently rewrite UX copy — e.g. drop the System Settings
recovery hint from `notAuthorized`, or strip the underlying message from
an interpolating case — without tripping any regression signal. This fire
closed those gaps for ChannelError, IMessageSender.SendError,
AppleScriptReaderError, and (on a polish wip branch) OAuthError, plus
pinned the `DraftState.isLowConfidence` threshold (0.4) that controls
when ComposerView shows the warning affordance, and pinned the
`Fixtures.demoChatThreads` seed shape that drives first-launch UX.

The OAuthError change is the only one shipping LocalizedError conformance
(not just tests) — pushed to wip-polish per autopilot policy because
SetChannelsView is UI-adjacent.
